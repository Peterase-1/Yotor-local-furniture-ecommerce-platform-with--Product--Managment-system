Our Story

Ethiopian Craftsmanship Since 2010

Founded in Addis Ababa by master carpenter Zenebe Kebede, ZENEBE Furniture began as a small workshop dedicated to preserving traditional Ethiopian woodworking techniques while adapting them to contemporary designs.

What started as a one-man operation has grown into a collective of over 50 skilled artisans across Ethiopia, each bringing their unique regional styles and expertise to our collections.

Today, we're proud to be one of Ethiopia's leading furniture makers, with our pieces gracing homes, offices, and hotels across the country and internationally.

Our Mission

To preserve and modernize Ethiopian craftsmanship while creating sustainable livelihoods for artisans across the country. We believe in furniture that tells a story - of the hands that made it, the materials that form it, and the home it will become part of.

Our Values

Sustainability

We use only ethically sourced materials from responsibly managed Ethiopian forests. All our finishes are non-toxic and environmentally friendly.

Craftsmanship

Each piece is handcrafted using traditional techniques passed down through generations, ensuring quality that lasts a lifetime.

Community

We invest in our artisans through fair wages, skill development, and opportunities for growth within our company.

Our Sustainability Commitment

Responsible Materials

We source all our wood from sustainably managed Ethiopian forests and plantations. For every tree used in our furniture, we plant three new ones through our partnership with local reforestation initiatives.

Our finishes are all water-based and non-toxic, safe for your family and the environment. The cotton we use is organically grown and handwoven by Ethiopian artisans.

Waste Reduction

In our workshops, nothing goes to waste. Wood scraps become smaller items like cutting boards or toys, sawdust is used for composting, and even our packaging materials are biodegradable or reusable.

We repair and restore furniture whenever possible, and offer a buy-back program for pieces you no longer need, which we refurbish or responsibly recycle.
